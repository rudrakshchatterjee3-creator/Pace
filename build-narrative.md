Aakash is preparing for the JEE. He sleeps five hours a night, feels guilty when he is not studying, and fears disappointing his parents. He is overwhelmed, spiraling after a bad mock test, and feeling completely alone. 

Before Pace, Aakash would either suppress these feelings or turn to generic productivity advice that only amplified his guilt. With Pace, he has a safe, non-judgmental space to unload his thoughts. Instead of a clinical therapy bot, Pace reflects his stressors back to him and gives him one concrete grounding strategy—like a five-minute breathing exercise—to help him regain his rhythm. 

Pace frames exam prep as a marathon rather than a sprint. We chose calm, muted colors like sage green and off-white instead of harsh clinical blues. We stripped away the visual noise of typical dashboards. Most importantly, we built a hard safety boundary: if the stress crosses into crisis territory, Pace immediately stops coaching and connects the student to real human helplines.
